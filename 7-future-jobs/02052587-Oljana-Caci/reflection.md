# Personal Reflection

After researching the future of software engineering jobs, I realized that AI will strongly influence the technology industry in the coming years. Before this research, I thought AI might completely replace programmers, but now I understand that the situation is more complex.

What surprised me the most is how quickly AI tools are improving. AI can already generate code, detect bugs, and help developers finish tasks much faster. This shows that software engineering will continue changing very rapidly.

One of my biggest concerns is that some developers may become too dependent on AI systems and stop improving their own knowledge. If engineers use AI-generated code without understanding it, software quality and security may become serious problems.

At the same time, I also see many opportunities. AI can remove repetitive tasks and allow developers to focus on more creative and important work. I believe future software engineers will spend more time solving complex problems, designing systems, and communicating with teams instead of writing simple repetitive code.

I think human skills such as critical thinking, communication, creativity, and decision making will remain very important because AI still cannot fully replace human understanding and judgment.

This research changed my opinion about AI. I no longer see AI as a replacement for software engineers. Instead, I see it as a powerful tool that developers must learn to use correctly.

Personally, I plan to adapt by continuously learning new technologies, improving my problem-solving skills, and understanding how AI tools work. I also want to strengthen my teamwork and communication skills because I believe these abilities will remain valuable in the future.
